              ⓘ
              How This Works — Read Before Entering Data
            

            

              
What This Page Does

              
The PTO Forecast estimates how many patient visits will be missed across each operating unit during the next three months based on how many provider PTO days are planned. It gives Ted and operations leadership a forward-looking view of expected volume impact — so staffing adjustments, coverage decisions, or patient scheduling changes can be made before the gap happens, not after.

            


            

              
How the Forecast is Calculated

              
The system pulls each entity’s last 12 weeks of actual visit data and computes a rolling average of clinical visits per day. That average is then divided by the total number of clinical providers at each location to get a per-provider daily rate. When you enter PTO days, the formula multiplies:

              

                
Provider TypeFormula

                
                  
MD PTO DaysMD PTO Days × (Clinical Visits/Day ÷ Total Clinical Providers)

                  
PA PTO DaysPA PTO Days × (Clinical Visits/Day ÷ Total Clinical Providers)

                  
PT PTO DaysPT PTO Days × (PT Visits/Day ÷ PT Provider Count)

                
              

              
The three impacts are summed to produce a Total Forecasted Missed Visits for that entity and month. The quarterly rollup adds all three months together.

            


            

              
Provider Counts Used

              
Provider counts per entity are configured in Admin → Provider Settings. Pods are counted as MDs. PRN staff are excluded. Current counts:

              

                
EntityMDsPAsPTs

                
                  
LAOSS2114—

                  
NES1214

                  
SpineOne232

                  
MRO843

                
              

              
If headcount changes, update Provider Settings — no code changes needed. The forecast will automatically use the updated counts on the next save.

            


            

              
Important Limitations to Know

              

                
LimitationWhat It Means

                
                  
MD and PA rates are equalThe formula currently assumes MDs and PAs see the same volume per day since we don’t have per-provider-type visit data. In practice MDs may see more. This is a conservative estimate.

                  
Based on a rolling 12-week averageThe baseline rate reflects recent history. If the last 12 weeks included unusual volume (holiday weeks, closures, heavy PTO), the per-provider rate may be slightly understated or overstated.

                  
Does not account for coverageIf a provider on PTO has their patients covered by another provider, actual missed visits will be lower than the forecast. The tool assumes no cross-coverage unless adjustments are made to the PTO days entered.

                  
Half-day increments supportedEnter 0.5 for a half day of PTO. The formula scales proportionally.

                
              

            


            

              
How to Use This Page

              
1. For each entity and each month in the rolling quarter, enter the total planned PTO days by provider type — MD, PA, and PT where applicable.

2. Click Save on each month card. The forecast result appears immediately below the inputs showing the estimated impact by provider type and the total.

3. The 3-Month Impact Forecast table at the bottom rolls up all entities and months into a quarterly summary for leadership review.

Section 1

              
Dashboard

              
The command center. Cross-entity performance for any selected time window — the first thing ops leadership should check every Monday morning.

            


            

              
Period Controls

              
Use these controls to define what time window you are looking at and what you are comparing against.

              

                
ControlWhat it does

                
                  
PeriodSets the time window: Last Week, MTD, Last Month, Rolling 4 Weeks, or Custom Range.

                  
Compare AgainstPrior Period compares to the same window one period back. Budget compares actuals to prorated monthly targets.

                  
Entity ScopeFilter to a single operating unit (LAOSS, NES, SpineOne, MRO) or view all four together.

                  
Quick PresetsThe Last Week / MTD / Rolling 4 pills instantly switch the period without using the dropdowns.

                
              

            


            

              
Top-Line Metric Cards

              
Eight cards giving the executive snapshot. Each shows the current period value and, where applicable, a movement arrow showing change vs. the comparison period.

              

                
MetricDefinition & Why It Matters

                
                  
Visit VolumeNew Patients + Surgeries + Established visits. The primary volume indicator. Green = above comparison, red = below.

                  
PT VisitsPhysical therapy visits seen for NES, SpineOne, and MRO. Not shown for LAOSS which has no PT program.

                  
Cash CollectedTotal cash received across entities in the period. This is cash collected, not billed revenue.

                  
PTO DaysProvider PTO taken in the period. Helps explain volume dips — if visits are down and PTO is up, that is your answer.

                  
Total SurgeriesSurgical cases performed. Tracked separately from visit volume to give surgical throughput visibility.

                  
Call VolumeTotal inbound calls. A leading indicator of demand and front-desk load.

                  
Avg CXNS %Combined average no-show and cancellation rate across entities. No Show and Cancel shown separately in the sub-label. Flag above 14% combined.

                  
Avg Abandoned %Percentage of inbound calls abandoned before being answered. Above 10% is a watchlist trigger.

                
              

            


            

              
Volume Trends Chart

              
Week-by-week movement across the selected period. Lines: Visits (blue), PT Visits (purple), New Patients (green), Calls (gold — hidden by default, click the legend to enable). In Budget mode, a red dashed line shows the visit volume target.

            


            

              
Entity Performance Cards

              
Each operating unit gets its own card showing: status chip, performance summary signal, visit/PT/cash for the period, variance vs. prior or budget progress bars, health metrics (No Show %, Cancel %, Abandoned %), and the Access Health bar.

            


            

              
Alerts & Wins

              
Alerts flag entities exceeding thresholds: no-show rate above 6%, cancellation rate above 8%, abandoned calls above 10%, or visits down more than 100 vs. prior period. Wins surface positive signals: entities above budget, PT improvement, strong call handling below 5% abandoned. Entity Snapshot is a quick data table of actuals for the selected scope.

            

          


          
          

            

              
Section 2

              
Weekly Entry

              
How each operating unit submits their operational data. Staff fill this in every Friday (or Monday for the prior week). The week ending date is always a Friday.

            


            

              
How it works

              
    
                
  • Select the entity and week ending date at the top. The entry audit panel shows whether a record already exists for that week.
    • 
                
  • The form auto-detects PT vs. standard entry and shows the correct fields.
    • 
                
  • Raw counts are entered. Rates (no-show %, cancellation %, abandoned call %) are calculated automatically — staff never type percentages.
    • 
                
  • Hit Save to store the draft. The entry is immediately visible in Trends and Activity Log.
    • 
              

            


            

              
Team Inputs (standard entry)

              

                
FieldWhat to enter

                
                  
New PatientsCount of new patient appointments seen this week.

                  
SurgeriesCount of surgical cases performed.

                  
EstablishedCount of established (returning) patient visits.

                  
No ShowsAppointments where the patient did not arrive and did not cancel.

                  
CancelledAppointments cancelled by the patient or clinic before the visit.

                  
Total CallsTotal inbound calls received by the front desk this week.

                  
Abandoned CallsCalls that rang but were hung up before being answered.

                  
Cash CollectedDollar amount of cash/copay collected at point of service this week.

                  
PTO DaysProvider PTO days taken during the week.

                
              

            


            

              
Calculated Fields (auto-computed, read-only)

              

                
FieldFormula

                
                  
Visit VolumeNew Patients + Surgeries + Established

                  
No Show %No Shows ÷ (Visit Volume + No Shows + Cancelled) × 100

                  
Cancellation %Cancelled ÷ (Visit Volume + No Shows + Cancelled) × 100

                  
Abandoned Call %Abandoned Calls ÷ Total Calls × 100

                
              

            


            

              
PT Inputs (PT entity entries only — NES-PT, SpineOne-PT, MRO-PT)

              

                
FieldWhat to enter

                
                  
PT Scheduled VisitsTotal PT appointments scheduled for the week.

                  
PT CancellationsPT appointments cancelled.

                  
PT No ShowsPT appointments where the patient did not arrive.

                  
PT ReschedulesPT appointments rescheduled (not cancelled outright).

                  
PT Total Units BilledTotal billing units generated by PT for the week.

                  
PT Visits SeenActual PT patient visits completed.

                  
PT Working DaysNumber of days PT operated this week (default 5).

                
              

            


            

              
Operator Notes

              
The free-text section at the bottom prompts for: what went well, what could be better, major red flags, adjustments for next week, and additional insight. These notes give leadership qualitative context behind the numbers and are stored with the weekly record.

            

          


          
          

            

              
Section 3

              
Executive Summary

              
Single-week deep dive across all four entities. Pick any week ending date and get a full roll-up. Designed for weekly leadership review calls.

            

            

              
What is shown

              
    
                
  • 10 top-line metric cards: Visit Volume, PT Visits, Cash Collected, PTO Days, Total Surgeries, Call Volume, New Patients, Avg No Show %, Avg Cancel %.
    • 
                
  • Approved Regions table: one row per entity — visits, cash, PTO, calls, new patients, and all three rate metrics side by side.
    • 
                
  • PT Productivity panel: PT totals and productivity metrics — units billed, units per visit, and a PT regional breakdown table for NES, SpineOne, and MRO.
    • 
              

            

          


          
          

            

              
Section 4

              
Trends

              
Historical week-over-week movement for a single entity. Spot seasonality, catch multi-week declines early, or validate that an operational change had the intended effect.

            

            

              
Controls

              

                
ControlOptions

                
                  
EntityLAOSS, NES, SpineOne, MRO — one entity at a time.

                  
Range ModeRecent Weeks shows the most recent N weeks of actual data. Date Range lets you specify start and end dates explicitly.

                  
Weeks8, 12, 16, 24, or 52 weeks of history in Recent mode. Auto-loads when changed.

                
              

            

            

              
What is shown

              
    
                
  • Summary cards: Weeks Loaded, Latest Visit Volume (with delta vs. prior week), Latest PT Visits, Latest Cash, Latest Call Volume.
    • 
                
  • Trend History table: week-by-week breakdown of visits, PT, cash, PTO, calls, no-show %, cancel %, and abandoned %.
    • 
                
  • PT Historical Performance panel: shown for NES, SpineOne, and MRO. Hidden for LAOSS which has no PT program.
    •

Section 6

              
Admin Import

              
Admin-only. Bulk-loads historical weekly data and budget targets from the locked operational workbook. Not for daily use — for backfilling history and onboarding new data.

            

            

              
What the import reads

              
    
                
  • LA, Portland, Denver, Chicago sheets — weekly visit, call, and cash data per entity.
    • 
                
  • PT sheet — per-entity PT scheduling, visits seen, and units billed.
    • 
                
  • MOR sheets — monthly provider PTO totals per entity.
    • 
                
  • Budget V. Monthly Results sheet — monthly visit and new patient targets for all four entities across all 12 months.
    • 
              

            

            

              
Important notes

              
    
                
  • The import is idempotent — running it twice with the same file produces the same result. Safe to re-run.
    • 
                
  • Budget targets are prorated daily, so a 5-week month distributes budget proportionally across each week.
    • 
                
  • PTO data comes from the MOR monthly summary tabs — these reflect whichever month the MOR tab is currently set to.
    • 
                
  • Weekly entry data submitted through the app takes precedence in the dashboard view over imported data.
    •

Glossary — Key Terms

              
Quick reference for every metric and term used across the dashboard.

            

            

              

                
TermDefinition

                
                  
Visit VolumeNew Patients + Surgeries + Established visits. The primary volume metric.

                  
No Show Rate% of scheduled appointments where the patient did not arrive and did not cancel. Flag above 6%.

                  
Cancellation Rate% of scheduled appointments cancelled before the visit. Flag above 8%.

                  
Abandoned Call Rate% of inbound calls hung up before being answered. Flag above 10%.

                  
Access HealthA composite signal (0–100%) derived from the abandoned call rate. Higher is better.

                  
CXNSCancellations and no-shows combined. CXNS % is the combined rate shown on the dashboard card.

                  
PT Visits SeenPhysical therapy patient visits actually completed in the week.

                  
Units BilledPT billing units generated. Each visit generates multiple units depending on modalities used.

                  
Units/VisitAverage PT billing units per visit. A PT productivity efficiency metric.

                  
Budget (prorated)Monthly budget targets divided proportionally across the working days in each week.

                  
Prior PeriodThe same-length window immediately before the current period. E.g., Last Week vs. the week before that.

                  
Week EndingAlways a Friday. The date that closes a Mon–Fri reporting week.

                  
MTDMonth to Date — accumulates all weeks with data from the 1st of the current month through today.

                  
Rolling 4The four most recent completed weeks, regardless of month boundaries.

                  
ApprovedA weekly entry that has been reviewed and marked approved by an administrator.

                  
Saved / DraftAn entry that has been entered and saved but not yet submitted or approved.

                  
PTO DaysProvider paid time off days taken in the period. Used to contextualize volume dips.

                  
LAOSSLos Angeles Orthopedic Surgery Specialists.

                  
NESNorthwest Extremity Specialists (Portland).

                  
SpineOneSpineOne (Denver).

                  
MROMidland & Riverside Orthopedics (Chicago).
